# Product

## Register

product

## Users

OpenReveal serves two people in one brief live moment. A performer needs to start a session, confirm that a spectator is connected, stage a reveal, and send it without hunting through a dashboard. A spectator opens a page by choice, enters a short code, and waits on their own phone. The spectator flow must remain quiet, private, and easy to exit.

## Product Purpose

OpenReveal is an open-source, consent-based spectator-phone reveal system. It makes a two-device performance feel immediate without requiring an app install, an account, or hidden access to a spectator device. Success is a clean routine from code entry to reveal, with the performer always understanding the live state and the spectator never being confused about what they opened.

## Brand Personality

The brand is theatrical, exacting, and calm. It should feel like a well-run stage cue rather than a magic-shop prop, generic SaaS tool, or imitation of another product. Public material can be expressive and memorable; live controls must communicate certainty in a glance.

## Anti-references

- Generic startup landing pages with interchangeable card grids and feature metrics.
- Faux search, browser, or phone interfaces that imitate third-party products.
- Busy magic aesthetics: playing-card ornament, smoke, top hats, mystical symbols, or novelty copy.
- Dense operator dashboards that make a simple live routine feel technical or risky.
- Spectator pages with marketing, navigation, links, or branding that can reveal the mechanism.

## Design Principles

1. The spectator path has one job at a time.
2. The performer should understand readiness without reading diagnostics.
3. Make the public story tangible by showing the real product, not invented promises.
4. Confidence comes from clear states, not decorative complexity.
5. Consent and control are part of the experience, not an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA for text, controls, focus states, and contrast. Support keyboard use, browser zoom, screen-reader labels, reduced motion, touch targets of at least 44px on mobile, safe-area insets, and clear error recovery. Motion is atmospheric only and must never block a live action.
